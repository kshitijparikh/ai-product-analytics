The Origin Story

Hi, I'm Kshitij. I'm a software engineer who became obsessed with a simple question:
"Why do some products become daily habits while others disappear?"

As an API developer, I spent years watching data move between systems. Eventually, I became much more interested in what happened after the data arrived—why users clicked, stayed, purchased, abandoned, binge-watched, or came back.

That curiosity turned me into a product analyst.

I have spent 3+ years working across engineering, experimentation, and product development at companies like Optimoz and Sigma Solve. I use Python, SQL, A/B testing, and analytics to transform raw user behavior into concrete product decisions.

A Strange Hobby

Outside of work, I reverse-engineer products that help people survive modern life. Not to review them, but to understand the psychology, metrics, incentives, onboarding flows, retention loops, and growth mechanisms that make millions of people return every day.

  • Food: Dissected Swiggy & Amazon.
  • Entertainment: Tore apart Netflix, Crunchyroll & Spotify.
  • Transit: Analyzed Uber.

My father always taught me that the best answers are usually hidden in the root cause. That philosophy shapes how I approach products: keep digging until the numbers tell a story.

Whether it's improving onboarding, increasing retention, designing experiments, identifying growth opportunities, or building analytics systems, I enjoy finding the "why" behind user behavior and turning it into measurable business impact.

If you're looking for someone who can think like an engineer, analyze like a product analyst, and occasionally obsess over a funnel drop-off rate more than is socially acceptable—you're in the right place.
