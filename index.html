The Origin Story

Hi, I'm Kshitij. I'm a software engineer who became obsessed with a simple question:
"Why do some products become daily habits while others disappear?"

As an API developer, I spent years watching data move between systems. Eventually, I became more interested in what happened after the data arrived—why users clicked, stayed, purchased, abandoned, binge-watched, or came back.

That curiosity turned me into a product analyst.

I have spent 3+ years working across engineering, experimentation, and product development at companies like Optimoz and Sigma Solve, using Python, SQL, A/B testing, and analytics to transform raw user behavior into product decisions.

A Strange Hobby

Outside of work, I reverse-engineer products that help people survive modern life:

  • Food? I dissected Swiggy and Amazon.
  • Entertainment? I tore apart Netflix, Crunchyroll, and Spotify.
  • Transportation? I analyzed Uber.

Not because I wanted to review them. Because I wanted to understand the psychology, metrics, incentives, onboarding flows, retention loops, and growth mechanisms that make millions of people return every day.

My father always taught me that the best answers are usually hidden in the root cause. That philosophy has shaped how I approach products: keep digging until the numbers tell a story.

Whether it's improving onboarding, increasing retention, designing experiments, identifying growth opportunities, or building analytics systems, I enjoy finding the "why" behind user behavior and turning it into measurable business impact.

If you're looking for someone who can think like an engineer, analyze like a product analyst, and occasionally obsess over a funnel drop-off rate more than is socially acceptable—you're in the right place.

The AARRR Growth Playbook: Deconstructing Tech Titans

Most product teardowns look at a single app in isolation. To truly understand what drives hyper-growth, I reverse-engineered the success of five industry titans through the lens of the Pirate Metrics (AARRR) framework. In this 5-part thesis, I explore how Uber engineered a psychologically addictive Acquisition engine, how Blinkit eliminates decision fatigue to drive Activation, how Netflix leverages personalized UI for Retention, how Amazon uses social proof to maximize Revenue, and how Spotify built an organic Referral loop. This project bridges the gap between behavioral psychology, UX design, and deep-funnel product analytics.
