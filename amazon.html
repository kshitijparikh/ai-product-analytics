User Segmentation & Personas

Buyers

Millions of individuals globally purchasing products and services. Amazon tracks customers based on characteristics such as interests, engagement, and personal information (age, gender, geographical location).

Sellers

Companies utilizing Amazon's e-commerce platform to reach a vast audience. Offer products to Amazon's wide customer base.

Developers (AWS)

Community associated with Amazon Web Services. Encompasses customers and partners across various industries and sizes (startups, enterprises, public sector).

A

Abhishek (Buyer)

15-year-old student residing in a tier 2 city in Maharashtra, preparing for competitive exams.

Pain Points

  • Unable to find required study materials in local bookstores.
  • Limited access to competitive exam books in his city.
  • Hassle of traveling to bigger cities to buy books, incurring extra costs and time.

Needs

  • Access to a wide variety of competitive exam books.
  • Convenient online platform to purchase study materials without extensive travel.
  • Affordable and timely delivery services to his tier 2 city.

Sayajirao (Seller)

40-year-old director of a leading book publication with 85% market share.

Pain Points

  • Inability to reach customers in tier 2 and tier 3 cities, limiting market expansion.
  • Losing potential customers due to the lack of availability in smaller cities.

Needs

  • An online platform with a vast reach, enabling her to sell books nationwide.
  • Access to a reliable e-commerce platform that caters to customers from tier 2 and tier 3 cities.
  • Efficient logistics and delivery services to ensure books reach customers promptly.

Customer Reviews: How it Works

Customer Feedback

Reviews are submitted by customers who have bought the product.

Star Ratings and Comments

Customers rate the product on a scale of 1 to 5 stars and provide written feedback.

Verification

Reviews can be verified (from confirmed buyers) or unverified (from customers who didn't buy through Amazon).

Helpful Votes

Other customers can mark reviews as helpful, guiding fellow shoppers.

Amazon Vine Program

Trusted reviewers receive products for honest feedback through the Vine program.

Moderation

Reviews are moderated to remove fake ones, ensuring authenticity.

Who Cannot Give Reviews on Amazon?

Biased review with financial interest. Personal ties to owner/artist. Manufacturer posing as shopper. Multiple negatives from one customer. Paid or rewarded reviews. In-game credits exchange for review. Competitor's negative review. Artist mutual praise for positive reviews.

User Journey: Writing a Review

Step 1
Select a product for review from Order Info.
Step 2
Give overall rating out of 5 stars.
Step 3-6
Click on add photo, capture the photo, and upload the photo.
Step 7-8
Write title for the review, write the review text, and click next.
Step 9-11
Give specific feature ratings (e.g. charging power, travelling, battery life) and submit.
Step 12
Review submitted! Can select next product to review.

Percentage of reviews marked useful by readers compared to total published reviews.
